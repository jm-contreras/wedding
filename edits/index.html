About Amor en Ruinas

Ruins remind us nothing lasts forever and what's worth holding onto is the people we choose to keep close. Before we begin our life together, we want to pause, among these centuries-old walls, to honor our love for each other and for everyone who shaped us.

Our story lives somewhere between Bolivia and the United States. We'll gather in the ruins of Santa Clara in Antigua to celebrate that we met, fell in love, and decided to spend the rest of our lives together.

What's the dress code?

Welcome Cocktail

Festive garden casual: a notch more relaxed, still put together.

  • Women: sundresses, linen dresses, or nice tops with pants or skirts.
  • Men: breathable button-downs or linen shirts with chinos or linen pants.

Wedding Day

Festive garden cocktail: think elevated semi-formal for a setting that's equal parts garden and ruins, breathable fabrics, vibrant colors, and shoes that move well over grass and cobblestones.

  • Women: midi, knee-length, or flowy maxi dresses, or jumpsuits. Skip stilettos; block heels, wedges, or dressy flats hold up far better outdoors.
  • Men: linen or lightweight suits, tie optional.

Bring a light layer for when the evening cools down.

Farewell Brunch

Casual: everyday clothes.

Will the wedding be in English?

Yes. The ceremony and all the key moments will be in English (and Spanish too), so you'll be able to follow everything. A bilingual program will guide you through the day.

Are kids welcome?

The wedding includes children from our immediate family, our first cousins, and our DJ's kids. To keep the celebration relaxed for everyone else, we've kept it adults-only otherwise.

Can I bring a plus-one?

Plus-ones are listed by name on your RSVP form. We kept the guest list intentionally close.

How do I get to the wedding venue?

The ceremony is at the ruins of the Convent of Santa Clara in central Antigua. Our travel guide covers flights, getting from the airport, where to stay, and getting around once you're there.
