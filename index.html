About Amor en Ruinas

Ruins remind us that nothing lasts forever and that what's worth holding onto is the people we choose to keep close. Before we begin our life together, we want to pause, among these centuries-old walls, to honor our love for each other and for everyone who shaped us.

Our story lives somewhere between Bolivia and the United States. At the ruins of Santa Clara in Antigua, we'll gather to celebrate that we met, that we fell in love, and that we've decided to spend the rest of our lives together.

What's the dress code?

For the wedding day: dressy but relaxed — what some call "garden cocktail." No suits, ties, tuxedos, or floor-length gowns required. For a sense of it: men can wear a button-down or linen shirt with slacks, or a light blazer; women a sundress, cocktail dress, or a nice blouse and skirt. Think a beautiful garden dinner party. Earth tones and florals suit the setting. The streets are cobblestone, so wear shoes you can walk in — leave the stiletto heels at home. Antigua's evenings get cool, so bring a light jacket or wrap. The welcome cocktail and farewell brunch are garden casual — relaxed, but a touch more put-together than everyday.

Will the wedding be in English?

Yes. The ceremony and all the key moments will be in English (and Spanish too), so you'll be able to follow everything. A bilingual program will guide you through the day.

Are kids welcome?

To let everyone relax and enjoy the celebration, the wedding is adults-only, with the exception of immediate family. We hope this gives parents a chance to enjoy a night out — thank you for understanding.
