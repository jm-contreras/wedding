About Amor en Ruinas

Ruins remind us that nothing lasts forever and that what's worth holding onto is the people we choose to keep close. Before we begin our life together, we want to pause, among these centuries-old walls, to honor our love for each other and for everyone who shaped us.

Our story lives somewhere between Bolivia and the United States. At the ruins of Santa Clara in Antigua, we'll gather to celebrate that we met, that we fell in love, and that we've decided to spend the rest of our lives together.

What's the dress code?

For the wedding day: festive garden cocktail. Think elevated semi-formal for a setting that's equal parts garden and ruins: breathable fabrics, vibrant colors, and shoes that move well over grass and cobblestones.

Women: midi, knee-length, or flowy maxi dresses, or a jumpsuit. Skip the stilettos; block heels, wedges, or dressy flats hold up far better outdoors. Men: a linen or lightweight suit, no tie required.

Bring a light layer for when the evening cools down.

The welcome cocktail is festive garden casual: a notch more relaxed, still put together. Women: a sundress, linen dress, or a nice top with trousers or a skirt. Men: a breathable button-down or linen shirt with chinos or linen trousers.

The farewell brunch is just casual: everyday clothes, nothing more needed.

Will the wedding be in English?

Yes. The ceremony and all the key moments will be in English (and Spanish too), so you'll be able to follow everything. A bilingual program will guide you through the day.

Are kids welcome?

The wedding includes children from our immediate family, our first cousins, and our DJ's kids. To keep the celebration relaxed for everyone else, we've kept it adults-only otherwise. We hope this gives other parents a chance to enjoy a night out, thank you for understanding.

Can I bring a +1?

We've extended plus-ones to guests who are married or in an existing relationship, and listed them by name on your RSVP form. If there's no plus-one field on your form, we weren't able to extend one for this wedding. We kept the guest list intentionally close, and we hope you understand.

How do I get to the wedding venue?

The ceremony is at the ruins of the Convent of Santa Clara in central Antigua. Our travel guide covers flights, getting from the airport, where to stay, and getting around once you're there.
